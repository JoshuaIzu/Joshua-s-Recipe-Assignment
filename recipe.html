Recipe: Egusi Soup

Egusi soup is a delicious and popular Nigerian dish made with ground melon seeds, vegetables and assorted meats.

Recipe Information:

  • Preparation Time: 30 minutes
  • Cooking Time: 1 hour
  • Servings: 4-6 people
  • Ingredients: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of Egusi (melon) seeds, ground
  • 1 kg of assorted meat(beef, tripe, goat meat)
  • 2 cups of stock or water
  • 2 tablespoons of ground crayfish
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • 1 cup of palm oil
  • 1 medium onion, chopped
  • 2 cups of vegetables (e.g. pumpkin leaves, spinach or bitter leaves)
  • 2 tablespoons of locust beans (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 teaspoon of ground pepper
  • 1 cup of dry fish or stockfish, cleaned

Instructions

  1. Prepare the Meat & Stock: Wash and season the assorted meat with salt, seasoning cubes, and chopped onions. Cook until tender, then set aside with the stock.
  2. Prepare the Egusi Paste: Mix the ground egushi with a little water to form a thick paste.
  3. Cook the Egusi: Heat palm oil in a pot and saute onions until fragrant. Add the egusi paste in small lumps and fry for about 5-7 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  4. Add Stock & Meat: Pour in the meat stock and stir well. Add the cooked meat, dry fish, crayfish, and locust beans. Allow to simmer for 10 minutes.
  5. Add Vegetables & Seasoning: Stir in the chopped vegetables and let cook for another 5 minutes. Adjust seasoning with salt and pepper.
  6. Serve & Enjoy: Serve hot with pounded yam, fufu, or eba.

Cooking Tip:

For a richer flavor, allow the soup to simmer longer, adjust the seasoning to taste and add more ground pepper. Best eaten with fufu, enjoy your meal!
